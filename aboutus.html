Sustainable Energy Transformation For
Tamil Nadu (SET-TN)

An initiative to facilitate, develop and support progressive policies and financial, technical and stakeholder engagement strategies for a sustainable energy transformation in the state of Tamil Nadu

Objectives

To facilitate policy dialogue and discussions to create progressive policy and regulatory frameworks (State and Centre) for sustainable energy solutions.
To engage and strengthen the technical capacity and knowledge of energy institutions in Tamil Nadu.
To build stakeholder. consensus through active engagement with Civil Society Organisations (CSOs), government, utilities, regulators, consumers and prosumers on the pathway to sustainable energy transformation.

Initiative Partners

SET is a collaborative initiative by World Resources Institute India (WRI India), Citizen Consumer and Civic Action Group (CAG) and Auroville Consulting (AVC).

Context

Tamil Nadu has been a leader in the renewable energy sector in India. To give a greater impetus to renewable energy, the Tamil Nadu Government has continued to provide various enabling policies e.g. the Solar Energy Policy 2019 has set a solar energy target of 9,000 MW by 2023, of which 40% has been allocated to consumer category (on-site systems). The Tamil Nadu Electricity Regulatory Commission (TNERC) determines annual tariffs for various renewable energy sources, such as wind, solar (utility/consumer) biomass etc. State Government agencies such as TANGEDCO and TEDA are playing a key role in the implementation and facilitation of renewable energy programs. Over the years some of these efforts faced operational, financial and technical issues. Today, learnings from these can be leveraged to accelerate Tamil Nadu’s sustainable energy transformation, which besides promoting renewable energy includes planning and development of smart grids, energy storage facilities, enhancement of energy conservation and efficiency measures and sustainable mobility solutions.
